About Leh-Ladakh

Leh, the capital of Ladakh, is a high-desert city in the Himalayas with stunning landscapes at an average elevation of 3,500m. This region is known for its Buddhist monasteries, adventure activities, and breathtaking mountain vistas.

Top Attractions

Pangong Tso

The famous high-altitude lake that changes colors from blue to green to red

Leh Palace

17th-century palace offering panoramic views of the town and surrounding mountains

Nubra Valley

High-altitude desert valley known for its sand dunes and Bactrian camels

Quick Facts

  • Best Time to Visit: May to September (other months have extreme cold)
  • Average Temperature: 15°C to 25°C (summer), -15°C to 5°C (winter)
  • Altitude Sickness: Acclimatization for 2 days recommended
  • Unique Experience: World's highest motorable roads

Travel Permits Required

Foreign nationals need Protected Area Permits (PAP) to visit Leh-Ladakh. Indian nationals require Inner Line Permits for certain areas.
